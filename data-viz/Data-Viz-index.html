New Army PT test could disproportionately impact black women
By Philip Van Slooten

ACFT Fails by Gender: 2019

The Army is moving forward with implementing a new "gender neutral" combat fitness test despite initial reports of a high failure rate by female troops.

Military.com reported in October the female failure rate was as high as 84 percent, according to "leaked slides" which revealed the results for 11 batallions consisting of over 3,000 mostly male personnel.

ACFT Events: (1) Deadlift; (2) Standing Power Throw; (3) Hand Release Push Up; (4) Sprint, Drag, Carry; (5) Leg Tuck and (6) 2-Mile Run.
Sources: Military.com and armytimes.com

A majority of the failures, 72 percent, were in the leg tuck event. Army-Fitness.com reports this event requires double the amount of force required for a traditional sit-up as soldiers must touch their knees or thighs to their elbows while remaining suspended from a pull-up bar.





Female Officers by Branch: 2016

The percentage of women in the military has increased from 2 to 18 since then end of the Vietnam War, according to the Council on Foreign Relations.

This corresponds with an increase in the percent of female officers and combat veterans in positions of civilian leadership, such as former Army Lt. Col. and current Sen. Tammy Duckworth (D-Illinois).



Current Army and Air Force ROTC cadets and officers at the University of Maryland agree low physical test scores can negatively impact retention and promotion across branches.

Source: cfr.org


Black Female Enlisted: 2016

More black women serve in the Army than in any other branch and would be disproportionatly affected by declines in recruitment, retention and promotion based on physical training performance.



Source: cfr.org
